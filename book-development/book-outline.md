# Working Book Outline

# Intentional Development

**From Domain Understanding to Deployed Features Through Shared Intent, Behavior, and Verification**

## Foreword / Introduction — Preserving Intent

Purpose:
Introduce the book's central philosophy: software development is a chain of translations, and every translation can introduce drift.

Core argument:
Modern methods such as DDD, BDD, testing, DevOps, observability, and AI parity testing are best understood as intent-preservation techniques.

---

# Part I — Understanding Drift

## Chapter 1 — The Software That Was Intended

Software rarely fails merely because code cannot be written. It fails because the delivered system diverges from what was intended.

## Chapter 2 — Specification Drift

Define specification drift and distinguish it from technical debt, poor communication, unclear requirements, and changing scope.

## Chapter 3 — Cognitive Load and Knowledge Compression

Software development as knowledge work: teams compress, translate, and reconstruct knowledge under cognitive constraints.

---

# Part II — Understanding Intent

## Chapter 4 — Domain Before Technology

Introduce the Feature before Technology philosophy. Domain understanding precedes technical solutioning.

## Chapter 5 — Language, Models, and Shared Meaning

DDD as an intent-preservation practice. Ubiquitous language, bounded contexts, and domain models.

## Chapter 6 — Graphs as Specification Instruments

Graphs as a way of representing relationships, dependencies, rules, responsibilities, and domain structures.

## Chapter 7 — Wireframes as Cognitive Bridges

Wireframes as lightweight instruments for surfacing assumptions about interaction, flow, state, and user intent.

---

# Part III — Expressing Intent

## Chapter 8 — Behavior as the Center of Specification

Behavior as the bridge between domain intent and technical implementation.

## Chapter 9 — BDD as Shared Storytelling

BDD as an intent-preservation method, not merely a testing technique.

## Chapter 10 — Executable Specifications

How examples, scenarios, and automation help preserve intent across planning, development, testing, and delivery.

---

# Part IV — Proving Intent

## Chapter 11 — Verification as Evidence

Testing, review, auditability, and monitoring as forms of evidence generation.

## Chapter 12 — Designing and Analysing Testing Processes

Testing processes as cognitive and organizational systems. Risk, coverage, automation, feedback, and maintainability.

## Chapter 13 — Continuous Delivery as Continuous Verification

CI/CD as a chain of proof rather than merely a deployment mechanism.

## Chapter 14 — Observability and Operational Drift

Production behavior as the final test of intent. Metrics, logs, traces, feedback, and operational learning.

---

# Part V — Intentional Development in the AI Era

## Chapter 15 — AI Changes Everything and Nothing

AI accelerates production but does not eliminate the need for intent, judgment, verification, and responsibility.

## Chapter 16 — AI Parity Driven Development

Parity testing for AI systems, model upgrades, agents, and AI-assisted workflows.

## Chapter 17 — Human Intent in Agentic Development

As AI systems generate more of the implementation, humans become more responsible for framing intent and validating outcomes.

# Part VI — Agile V

## Chapter 18 — Agile V as a Lightweight Cognitive Work Model

Present Agile V as the operating model that ties the philosophy together.

## Chapter 19 — Collaboration in Agile V

Team-wide domain planning, pair-level specification, focused implementation, paired verification, and team-wide proof and delivery.

## Chapter 20 — Running Feature-by-Feature Development

How to apply Agile V in practical feature delivery.

## Chapter 21 — Scaling Intentional Development

How the model works across teams, products, organizations, consultancies, and AI-assisted environments.

## Chapter 22 — The Future of Software Engineering

The future is not only writing software faster. It is preserving intent more accurately.
